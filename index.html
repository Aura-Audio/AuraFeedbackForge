LFO Rate Zones

  • Below 20 Hz (above 0.05s): True LFO. Ear perceives rhythmic volume pulsing — tremolo, gating, breathing effects.
  • 20–100 Hz (0.05s–0.01s): Transition zone. Pulsing becomes too fast to distinguish individually. Sounds like roughness/buzz.
  • 100–1000 Hz (0.01s–0.001s): Amplitude modulation synthesis. Sidebands appear at signal_freq ± modulator_freq. Metallic, ring-modulated timbres. The "LFO" is now an audible tone shaping the signal.

0.0001s (10 kHz) is excluded — at that rate the modulator exceeds Nyquist for most content and produces aliasing noise, not modulation.
